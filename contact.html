    WORK IN PROGRESS

    • Two functions of attitude reports: prediction and explanation.

      • In recent years, ascriber-sensitivity of attitude reports has been appealed establish some striking conclusions about attitude ascriptions. First, Ethan Jerzak (2018) has argued that ‘want’ is a specification of ‘ought’. Second, Blumberg and Lederman (2020) have claimed that attitude reports do not report attitudes. Finally, Blumberg and Holguin (2018) have suggested that a certain class of ascriber-sensitive reports is a counterexample to descriptivism. In this paper I argue that these arguments overlook the fact that attitude reports have at least two distinct, albeit connected, functions: explanation and prediction of ascribee’s actions. I claim that taking attitude reports to be polysemous enables us to accommodate the ascriber-sensitivity data, while avoiding the revisionary conclusions reached by Blumberg, Holguin, Jerzak, and Lederman.
      keywords: attitude reports, semantics/pragmatics

    • Homonymy is not to be multiplied beyond necessity.

      • Recent critics of Grice’s Modified Occam’s Razor, “Senses are not to be multiplied beyond necessity”, focused excessively on “necessity” and insufficiently on “senses.” In this paper I defend a version of the principle, according to which “Distinct senses (homonyms) are not to be multiplied beyond necessity.” Furthermore, I put forward the hypothesis that this is actually the intended reading of Grice’s razor.
      keywords: Paul Grice
